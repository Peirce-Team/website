Principal Investigators

The “Peirce Interprets Peirce” project is powered by a distinguished team of interdisciplinary scholars, uniting expertise in semiotics, digital humanities, and computational linguistics.

Davide Picca
PI

X

Davide Picca is a professor and researcher in digital humanities and artificial intelligence at the University of Lausanne. His work focuses on the intersection between language, semiotics and AI, based on the principle that linguistic models are not mere computer machines, but genuine tools for interpreting culture and thought. During his career, he has been a visiting professor at the University of Turin and the Sorbonne in Paris, and is currently a research fellow at Harvard University's metaLAB, where he studies the relationship between digital technologies and the humanities. He is also a scientific member of the Umberto Eco Foundation, where he coordinates aspects related to technology and artificial intelligence, with the aim of improving archives, libraries and collections through advanced digital tools.

Irene Mittenberg
Co-PI
X

Irene Mittelberg is Professor of Linguistics and Cognitive Semiotics at the Institute for English, American, and Romance Studies of RWTH Aachen University, where she directs the Centre for Sign Language and Gesture (SignGes) and founded the Cognitive Semiotics Lab, a gesture research lab equipped with motion-capture technology. She holds an M.A. in French linguistics and art history from Hamburg University and a Ph.D. in linguistics and cognitive studies from Cornell University.

Research Team

Beyond the core leadership, a diverse team of postdoctoral researchers, PhD candidates, and technical specialists are actively engaged in every facet of the project, from meticulous manuscript transcription to the development of interactive digital tools. Their collective efforts are crucial to transforming Peirce's historical archives into dynamic, accessible resources for the 21st century.

Sebastian Feil
PostDoc

X

Sebastian Feil is a post-doctoral researcher at RWTH Aachen with the department of English Studies in the research area Linguistics and Cognitive Semiotics. He hold a Master’s degree in Comparative Literature, American Literature and Philosophy from the University of Augsburg and has completed a PhD in Philosophy with Prof. Helmut Pape at the University of Hildesheim on the concept of the preconceptual with particular attention to American Pragmatism and the concept of habit in the epistemology and semiotics of Charles Sanders Peirce. He has worked on the relationship of Peirce’s philosophy and semiotics and contemporary continental philosophy (Gadamer, Foucault, Blumenberg) and Peirce’s concept of abduction. Sebastian Feil serves as a member of the Advisory Committee of the German Semiotics Society (DGS).seb homepageseb researchgate

Carlo Teo Pedretti
Ph.D. Candidate

X

Carlo Teo Pedretti is a PhD candidate in Heritage Science at La Sapienza University of Rome, and holds an MA in Philosophical Sciences and Digital Humanities from the University of Bologna. His research applies semantic web technologies to model scholarly hermeneutical processes, focusing on the act of annotation. Currently, he uses visual semiotics and VLMs to interpret diagrams in historical manuscripts.

Lorenzo Zangari
PostDoc

Lorenzo Zangari earned his Ph.D. in Information and Communication Technologies in February 2025 at the Department of Computer Engineering, Modeling, Electronics, and Systems Engineering (DIMES), University of Calabria, Italy. He graduated magna cum laude in Computer Engineering in 2020.
